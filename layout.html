OLYMPIC GAMES FROM 1896-2014

The Olympic Games is an internationally celebrated sporting event that occurs every four years. It alternates between the Summer and Winter games and is regarded as one of the most prestigious global athletic competitions. Its roots trace back to ancient Greece, with the first Olympic Games taking place in Olympia in 776 BC. In the modern era, the Olympic Games include a wide array of sports and disciplines, ranging from track and field to swimming, gymnastics, ball sports and much more. Ranking the top 3 contender/s with a Gold medal, Silver medal and Bronze medal. Beyond athletic achievement, the Olympic Games serve as a platform for athletes to demonstrate their skills on a worldwide scale, and represent their home country against the world.

As shown in the interactive map Figure 1, the medal distribution spread far and wide. The United states of America is shown to have won the most Gold Medals with a staggering 1173 Medals. Germany comes in second with the United Kingdom, China, France and Italy not too far behind.

Figure 2 below shows the medal distribution of just the summer olympics. Again the United states of America is leading in Gold medals for the summer olympics. However as shown Figure 3, while the United states of America is still a close second, Norway leads the way in winter olympic Gold medals with 148 medals.

Note: Some countries that participated in the earlier years will not have a location on the maps in Figures 1, 2 and 3 due to the country's no longer existing, such as the Soviet Union and Czechoslovakia.

Figure 4 shows the distribution of olympic medals with comparison to the country's Population and GDP per Capita. The United States is shown to have the best of both worlds, a considerable Population (3rd highest) along with a considerable GPD per Capita (6th highest). However, Luxembourg shows that a high GDP per Capita doesn't guarantee medals with only 5 medals. India also shows that a high Population does not necessarily mean they perform well in the Olympics with only 35 total medals.

Note: Some countries that participated in the earlier years will not have a current GDP or Population due to them no longer existing, such as the Soviet Union and Czechoslovakia.

Figure 5 again shows the USA's domination with 2959 total Olympic medals. Comprising of: 1173 Gold medals, 953 Silver medals, and 833 Bronze medals. This chart however does include the Soviet Union which participated in the Olympics from 1952 to 1988. Obtaining a total of 1204 Olympic medals. Consisting of: 473 Gold medals, 376 Silver medals, and 355 Bronze medals. It is a lot closer between then next three and is debatable to which would be the 3rd most successful country as the United Kingdom boasts a total of 950 medals which is the next highest, however Germany has accrued more Gold medals sitting at 305 Gold medals compared to the United Kingdom's 296 Gold medals. Making them arguably the third most successful country at the Olympic Games.

Figure 7 shows the top performing athletes throughout the history of the Winter Olympic Games.
